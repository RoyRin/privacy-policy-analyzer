You are a privacy policy analyst. Grade the following privacy policy using these 15 criteria. Be strict and evidence-based — only give high scores when the policy explicitly commits to good practices. If the policy is vague or silent on a topic, that should count against it.

For each criterion, select the level that best matches the policy. You MUST pick one of the listed levels — do not interpolate between them.

## HANDLING (how the company handles your data)

1. **Behavioral Marketing** — Does the policy allow personally-targeted or behavioral marketing? (max 10 points)
   - 10: Policy explicitly prohibits all behavioral marketing and ad targeting
   - 7: Behavioral marketing exists but is opt-in (users must actively enable it)
   - 5: Behavioral marketing exists but users can fully opt out
   - 3: Behavioral marketing exists with only partial opt-out (e.g. can't opt out of all tracking)
   - 0: Pervasive behavioral marketing with no opt-out, or policy is silent on the topic

2. **Data Selling** — Does the company sell or license personal data to third parties? (max 10 points)
   - 10: Policy explicitly states it never sells or licenses personal data
   - 7: Does not sell personal data but may share it with affiliates or corporate family
   - 5: Sells or shares only aggregated or de-identified data
   - 3: Sells personal data but offers an opt-out mechanism
   - 0: Sells personal data with no opt-out, or policy is silent on data sales

3. **Third-Party Sharing** — Does the service share personal data with third parties? (max 10 points)
   - 10: No sharing beyond what is strictly necessary to provide the service (e.g. payment processor)
   - 7: Shares with specified third parties for limited, specified purposes; all parties named
   - 5: Shares with third parties for service operation but not all parties or purposes are specified
   - 3: Broad sharing with categories of third parties (e.g. "business partners," "affiliates")
   - 0: Unrestricted sharing, or third parties and purposes are unspecified

4. **Law Enforcement Access** — Under what conditions does the company share data with law enforcement? (max 5 points)
   - 5: Only complies with valid court orders; policy explicitly limits voluntary disclosure
   - 3: Complies with subpoenas and formal legal process only
   - 1: May voluntarily share data with law enforcement when it deems appropriate (e.g. "to protect safety")
   - 0: No policy on law enforcement access, or blanket willingness to cooperate

5. **Data Retention** — How long does the company keep your personal data? (max 5 points)
   - 5: Specifies concrete retention periods for each data type with automatic deletion
   - 3: States a general retention policy (e.g. "as long as your account is active") but no specific timeframes
   - 1: Mentions retention but is vague (e.g. "as long as necessary")
   - 0: No retention policy stated

## USER CONTROL (what power you have over your data)

6. **Data Deletion** — Can users permanently delete their personal data? (max 10 points)
   - 10: Self-service automated deletion (e.g. delete account button) that removes all personal data
   - 7: Self-service deletion available but some data may be retained (e.g. for legal obligations) with clear explanation
   - 5: Deletion available by contacting support; company commits to completing requests
   - 3: Deletion available by contacting support but with significant limitations or delays
   - 0: No deletion mechanism described, or policy says data cannot be deleted

7. **Opt-Out of Non-Critical Use** — Can users opt out of non-essential data use like analytics, marketing, and profiling? (max 10 points)
   - 10: All non-critical data use requires opt-in; nothing is on by default
   - 7: Opt-out available for all non-critical uses (marketing, analytics, profiling)
   - 5: Opt-out available for some non-critical uses (e.g. marketing emails) but not others (e.g. analytics)
   - 3: Only cookie/tracking opt-out available; no control over other non-critical uses
   - 0: No opt-out mechanisms described

8. **Consent Model** — How does the company obtain consent for data collection? (max 5 points)
   - 5: Explicit opt-in required for each type of data use; granular consent controls
   - 3: Opt-in required for sensitive data; other collection assumed by using the service
   - 1: All consent assumed by using the service or agreeing to terms
   - 0: Policy does not address consent, or uses pre-checked boxes / dark patterns

9. **Data Portability** — Can users export or transfer their data? (max 5 points)
   - 5: Self-service export in standard machine-readable formats (e.g. JSON, CSV)
   - 3: Export available by request; company provides data in a usable format
   - 1: Can request a copy of data but no standard format guaranteed
   - 0: No export or portability mechanism described

## TRANSPARENCY (how open the company is about its practices)

10. **Readability** — Is the policy written in clear, plain language? (max 5 points)
    - 5: Short (under ~2000 words), plain language, well-organized with clear headings and summaries
    - 3: Reasonably readable but lengthy, or uses some legal jargon
    - 1: Long, dense, heavy legalese, or poorly organized
    - 0: Deliberately obfuscating, contradictory, or incomprehensible

11. **Data Breach Notification** — Does the policy commit to notifying users of data breaches? (max 7 points)
    - 7: Commits to notifying affected users within 72 hours of discovering a breach
    - 5: Commits to notifying affected users without a specific timeframe
    - 3: Commits to notifying regulators but not necessarily individual users
    - 0: No breach notification commitment, or policy is silent on breaches

12. **Policy Change Notification** — How are users notified of policy changes? (max 5 points)
    - 5: Users notified in advance (e.g. 30 days) with ability to review changes before they take effect
    - 3: Users notified when changes take effect (e.g. email or banner notification)
    - 1: Policy says "check back periodically" or only updates the last-modified date
    - 0: No mention of how users will learn about changes

13. **Policy History** — Is the policy's revision history available? (max 3 points)
    - 3: Full changelog with diffs or detailed descriptions of changes
    - 2: Previous versions of the policy are archived and accessible
    - 1: Only a "last updated" date is shown
    - 0: No version history or date information

## COLLECTION & SECURITY (what's collected and how it's protected)

14. **Data Minimization** — Does the company collect only what it needs? (max 10 points)
    - 10: Collects only the bare minimum data required for the service to function; no non-essential collection
    - 7: Collects mostly necessary data with a small amount of non-essential data (e.g. basic analytics)
    - 5: Collects a moderate amount of non-essential data (e.g. device info, usage patterns, location)
    - 3: Collects extensive data including sensitive categories (e.g. biometrics, health, financial) beyond core service needs
    - 0: Collects data far beyond what the service needs, or collection scope is undefined

15. **Encryption & Security Practices** — Does the policy describe how user data is protected? (max 10 points)
    - 10: End-to-end encryption for user data; independent security audits; specific security measures described
    - 7: Strong encryption described (e.g. AES-256, TLS); regular security audits mentioned
    - 5: Mentions encryption and general security practices without specifics
    - 3: Vague references to "industry standard" security or "reasonable safeguards"
    - 0: No mention of security practices
